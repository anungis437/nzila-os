**Defining the moment when a venture, product, or service can be handed off, paused, or exited**

---

### **1. Purpose**

This guide defines the structured criteria and workflows used to:

- **Transition** ventures or service components from incubation to external ownership or automation
- **Pause** or **sunset** initiatives that no longer meet Nzilaâ€™s strategic, financial, or mission fit
- Ensure clarity in **IP ownership, continuity planning**, and **team reallocation**
- Maintain **institutional memory** and minimize risk during venture handoff

---

### **2. Handoff Types**

| Type | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| **Spinout** | Venture transitions to external legal entity with licensed IP, independent board, and capital structure |
| **Internal Sustain** | Venture/product continues under Nzila with reduced oversight, integrated into core systems |
| **Automation / Archive** | Process or tool is stable and either self-operating or archived with retrieval SOPs |
| **Exit / Sunset** | Venture or project ends with closure plan, IP wrap-up, and audit logs in place |

---

### **3. Minimum Criteria for Handoff or Exit**

| Area | Criteria |
| --- | --- |
| **IP Ownership** | All code, content, and IP licensed or reassigned |
| **Data Governance** | Data archived or transferred with consent logs and audit records |
| **Documentation** | Admin, technical, and user documentation complete and stored in Notion |
| **Team Transition** | Shared services disengaged or reassigned; venture hires or automation confirmed |
| **Stakeholder Notification** | Partners, funders, or caregivers notified with approved messaging |
| **Post-Handoff Plan** | Successor roadmap or archive plan created with review checkpoints |
| **Financial Closeout** | Budget, ledger entries, and capital usage reconciled |
| **Legal & Compliance** | Spinout paperwork, NDAs, and IP terms filed and signed |
