### 1. **Objective of the Policy**

The **Board Continuity & Observer Policy** ensures that Nzila Ventures' Board of Directors operates with a strong, diverse, and informed group of individuals who provide strategic oversight and guidance. The policy outlines practices for maintaining **board continuity**, addressing **board succession**, and introducing **observers** to provide additional perspectives without direct voting rights.

---

### 2. **Board Continuity Framework**

### A. **Board Composition & Tenure**

- **Director Composition**:
    
    The Board will consist of **5â€“7 Directors**, with a mix of **executive** and **non-executive** members, ensuring expertise in areas such as technology, finance, strategy, legal, and healthcare.
    
- **Term Limits**:
    - Each Board member shall serve for a **term of 3 years**, after which they will be subject to re-election or replacement.
    - Directors may serve up to **two consecutive terms** (6 years), after which they must step down for at least one year before being eligible for re-election.
- **Rotation**:
    - To ensure continuity while bringing fresh perspectives, at least one-third of the Board should rotate every 2â€“3 years.
    - **Succession Planning**: The Board will engage in **succession planning** to maintain a healthy balance between experienced directors and new voices. A **Board Succession Committee** will be formed to oversee director appointments and identify potential candidates.

### 3. **Board Observer Policy**

### A. **Purpose of Board Observers**

- **Strategic Involvement**:
    
    The Board may invite **observers** to attend Board meetings to ensure diverse insights, gain expertise in specific areas, or build relationships with future potential Board members.
    
- **Stakeholder Engagement**:
    
    Observers may be invited from **investors**, **partners**, or **advisors** to foster engagement with key stakeholders who have a vested interest in the companyâ€™s long-term success.
    

### B. **Eligibility of Observers**

- **Criteria for Selection**:
    - **Investors**: Strategic investors or major shareholders who have a financial or operational stake in the company.
    - **Advisors**: Subject matter experts who can provide additional value to the Board (e.g., legal, technology, finance, healthcare).
    - **Future Board Candidates**: Individuals identified as potential Board members for **future consideration**.
- **Appointment Process**:
    - Board Observers will be nominated by the **Nomination and Governance Committee** and approved by the **Board of Directors**.
    - The number of Board Observers at any given time will be capped at **2â€“3** to maintain efficiency during meetings.

### C. **Role of Observers**

- **Non-Voting**:
    
    Observers **do not have voting rights** and cannot influence official Board decisions.
    
- **Information Access**:
    
    Observers will receive the same **meeting materials** as Board members and will have access to Board discussions.
    
- **Meeting Attendance**:
    
    Observers may attend **Board meetings** but are excluded from **executive sessions** or discussions involving sensitive internal matters unless explicitly invited.
    
- **Feedback Mechanism**:
    
    Observers will provide **non-binding feedback** to the Board regarding strategic decisions or offer expert advice within their areas of expertise.

### 4. **Board Succession and Continuity Practices**

### A. **Identification of Successors**

- **Regular Review**:
    
    The **Nomination and Governance Committee** will conduct an annual review of the Boardâ€™s composition and the potential need for succession. Key factors will include:
    
    - Retirement or term expiration of existing Directors.
    - Changes in company needs (e.g., entering a new vertical or market).
    - Strengthening Board diversity (skills, experience, gender, ethnicity).
- **Diversity Goals**:
    
    Nzila Ventures is committed to ensuring the **diversity of the Board**, including **gender, background, and expertise**. The **Board Diversity Policy** will be reviewed annually to set concrete goals for achieving a balanced representation.
    

### B. **Emergency Succession Planning**

- **Contingency for Director Vacancy**:
    
    In the event of an unexpected **vacancy on the Board** (due to death, incapacity, or resignation), the **Nomination and Governance Committee** will identify an interim successor within **30 days** and initiate the process to fill the vacancy permanently.
    
- **Interim Leadership**:
    
    If the **Chairperson** or **CEO** role is vacated suddenly, an interim leader will be appointed by the **Board of Directors** to oversee critical functions until a permanent replacement is appointed.

### A. **Annual Board Review**

- **Self-Assessment**:
    
    The Board will conduct an **annual self-assessment** to evaluate its performance, identify areas for improvement, and assess the effectiveness of its governance practices.
    
- **Third-Party Evaluation**:
    
    Every 2â€“3 years, an independent third-party evaluator may be hired to assess the Boardâ€™s performance, effectiveness, and governance practices. This ensures an objective view of the Boardâ€™s functioning and areas for potential enhancement.
    

### B. **Feedback Process**

- **Director Feedback**:
    
    Board members will complete **anonymous surveys** to provide feedback on the overall effectiveness of meetings, Board dynamics, and any areas of concern.
    
- **Observer Feedback**:
    
    Observers will be asked to provide their insights on how they perceive the Board's decision-making processes and any suggestions for improvement.
