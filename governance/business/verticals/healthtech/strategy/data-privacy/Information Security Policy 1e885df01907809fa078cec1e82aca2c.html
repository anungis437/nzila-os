1. Introduction

The purpose of this Information Security Policy is to protect Nzila Ventures' information, technology, and intellectual assets. The policy is designed to ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information across all platforms, services, and products used by the company. This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third-party vendors who interact with Nzila Ventures' systems and data.

This policy adheres to internationally recognized standards and frameworks to ensure a robust information security posture and compliance with legal and regulatory requirements. Nzila Ventures is committed to fostering a culture of security and data protection across all its operations.

Aligned Frameworks:

  • ISO/IEC 27001 (Information Security Management Systems)
  • NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)
  • CIS Controls (Center for Internet Security Critical Security Controls)
  • GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)

2. Information Security Objectives

The Information Security Policy is designed to achieve the following objectives:

  1. Confidentiality: Ensure that sensitive data is protected from unauthorized access or disclosure.
  1. Integrity: Maintain the accuracy and trustworthiness of data by preventing unauthorized modification.
  1. Availability: Ensure that information and systems are accessible to authorized users when needed.
  1. Compliance: Meet regulatory requirements and align with industry best practices to ensure legal and regulatory compliance.

3. Scope

This policy applies to:

  • All data (both digital and physical), including customer, partner, and employee data.
  • All systems, including cloud services, on-premises infrastructure, and endpoints (laptops, mobile devices, etc.).
  • Employees, contractors, and third parties who have access to or manage Nzila Ventures' information and systems.
  • All technologies involved in processing or transmitting Nzila Ventures' data.

4. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Executive Management: Responsible for establishing and maintaining an effective security framework and ensuring the necessary resources for information security.
  • Information Security Officer (ISO): Oversees the implementation and enforcement of this policy and ensures alignment with security frameworks.
  • Employees: All employees and contractors must adhere to the security policies and practices, participate in regular security training, and report any security incidents.

5. Information Security Principles

  • Confidentiality: Only authorized personnel will have access to sensitive data.
  • Integrity: Data will be validated and verified to maintain accuracy and reliability.
  • Availability: Systems and data will be protected against downtime and data loss.
  • Compliance: All applicable laws, standards, and regulations will be adhered to.

6. Information Security Controls
