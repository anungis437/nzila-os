4. Initiatives by Pillar

‚úÖ Green Infrastructure

  • Host services on carbon-neutral cloud providers (AWS Sustainable Zones, Azure Canada Central)
  • Use energy-efficient AI models (distilled LLMs, edge AI when possible)
  • Implement usage-aware throttling in backend systems to avoid wasteful compute
  • Offset carbon from AI training runs through vetted climate initiatives

üåê Inclusive Access

  • Launch low-bandwidth modes for PuzzleLoop, MyLearning Companion, and OptivaCare
  • Ensure all products meet WCAG 2.2 AA standards for accessibility
  • Provide offline-first capability for partner clinics and schools with poor connectivity
  • Localize UI in French, Spanish, and Indigenous languages where relevant

üßò Digital Well-being

  • Embed rest state prompts and breaks into all gamified systems (e.g., PuzzleLoop)
  • Limit push notification frequency and allow user-controlled AI engagement thresholds
  • Apply cognitive load balancing design in interfaces for seniors, caregivers, youth
  • Ensure transparent AI behavior, avoid parasocial dependency loops

ü§ñ Responsible AI

  • All AI systems require bias audits and explainability reviews prior to deployment
  • Companion models must pass a non-manipulative design checklist
  • Maintain a Memory Graph opt-out policy with irreversible data deletion upon request
  • AI outputs must include confidence indicators and human override pathways

‚ù§Ô∏è Social Impact

  • Prioritize partnerships that serve public, community, or hybrid benefit orgs
  • Co-design initiatives with caregivers, teachers, and neurodiverse individuals
  • Measure impact in non-financial metrics (well-being improvements, time saved, caregiver relief)
  • Create an equity-based pricing model for lower-income or public institutions
